About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate specializing in machine learning, deep learning, and computer vision, with a strong focus on biomedical image analysis. My research develops data-efficient and reliable medical AI through domain adaptation, vision-language models, and active learning— enabling systems that adapt to domain shifts and reduce annotation burdens. I also develop segmentation, tracking, and visualization pipelines for high-dimensional microscopy data.

Selected Research Projects

DEGAST3D — 3D Graph-Based Plant Cell Tracking

Learning deformable 3D graph similarity for cell tracking in unregistered CLSM microscopy images.

ODES — Online Domain Adaptation with Expert Guidance

A source-free, single-pass online adaptation framework integrating expert-guided active learning and pseudo-label pruning to handle domain shifts in medical segmentation.

LINGUAL — Language-Guided Active Learning

A novel VLM-powered annotation refinement method that converts natural-language instructions into executable segmentation refinement programs, reducing annotation time by ~80%.

Plant Cell Analysis (3D + Time)

3D segmentation, reconstruction, registration, and spatio-temporal tracking of plant cells from CLSM imaging.

Medical VQA with Vision-Guided Verification

Integrating visual reasoning tools into chain-of-thought pipelines for more reliable clinical VQA.
